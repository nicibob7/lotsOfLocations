Candombe

Candombe is a traditional Afro-Uruguayan music and dance style that originated in Uruguay, particularly in the city of Montevideo. Candombe is characterized by its distinctive rhythm, which is produced by a set of three types of drums: chico, repique, and piano drums. These drums are played in a synchronized manner to create intricate and syncopated rhythms that are the foundation of the music. The rhythms are accompanied by chanting, singing, and dancing.

No Te Va Gustar

No Te Va Gustar (often abbreviated as NTVG) is a popular rock band from Uruguay. Formed in 1994 in Montevideo, the band has gained significant recognition and a dedicated fan base not only in Uruguay but also in other Spanish-speaking countries. The band's music is known for its diverse style, blending elements of rock, ska, reggae, and other genres. Their lyrics often touch on social and political themes, and they have been praised for their thought-provoking and introspective songwriting.

Murga

Murga is a vibrant and unique musical and theatrical genre that originated in Uruguay, specifically in the city of Montevideo. It is deeply ingrained in the country's cultural fabric and has a rich history dating back to the 19th century. Murga combines elements of music, singing, dance, and theater to create a dynamic and often satirical performance. It typically involves a group of performers, known as murgueros, who come together to create and present their murga during carnival season and other festivities.

Edouardo Mateo

Eduardo Mateo (1940-1990) was a highly influential Uruguayan musician, composer, and songwriter known for his innovative approach to music and his contributions to various genres, including rock, folk, and candombe. Mateo was a versatile artist who played guitar, sang, and experimented with different musical styles, often blending them together in unique ways. He was a key figure in the Uruguayan music scene and left a lasting impact on both musicians and audiences.
